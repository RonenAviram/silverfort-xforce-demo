Architecture Overview

A microservices enrichment pipeline — every source is an independent connector that feeds a unified brief engine
sales-intelligence/ ├── orchestrator/ — Central engine: receives AE request, fans out to connectors, assembles brief │ ├── brief-engine — Merges enriched data into structured brief with 5 sections │ ├── priority-ranker — Scores use cases by deal stage, industry, and past win patterns │ └── agenda-builder — Generates meeting agenda from Gong top-performer analysis │ ├── connectors/ — 6 independent data source adapters, each with its own auth and rate limits: │ ├── salesforce — Account data, deal stage, stakeholders, pipeline history │ ├── zoominfo — Org chart, company intel, tech stack, recent news │ ├── atlassian — Jira tickets (use case evidence), Confluence (competitive docs) │ ├── gong — Call transcripts, objection patterns, winning talk tracks │ ├── slack — Deal room context, SE/CSM signals, internal sentiment │ └── web-intel — Recent news, earnings, security incidents, job postings │ ├── competitive/ — Battle card engine maintained by Product Marketing: │ ├── comparison-matrix — Feature-by-feature scoring vs. CrowdStrike, Okta, CyberArk, Ping │ └── objection-bank — "They say X / You say Y" from top-performing Gong calls │ ├── feedback-loop/ — Post-meeting learning system: │ ├── outcome-tracker — Captures win/loss/stall + what landed per brief │ └── model-trainer — Re-weights use case ranking and objection relevance from outcomes │ ├── export/ — Output formats: │ ├── pdf-generator — Branded meeting brief PDF with sections + stakeholder map │ └── slack-digest — Auto-posts brief summary to deal room before meeting │ └── dashboard/ — Management visibility layer: ├── adoption-tracker — Usage by team, region, deal stage └── impact-analyzer — Win rate correlation, prep time reduction, feedback trends
